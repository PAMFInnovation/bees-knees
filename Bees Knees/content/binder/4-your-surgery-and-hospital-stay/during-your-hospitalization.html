During your hospitalization

You can help by:

  • Discussing your pain control options with your healthcare provider.
  • Letting your healthcare providers know as soon as you begin having pain.
  • Taking your pain medication at regular times. Most pain medication taken by mouth needs at least 20 to 30 minutes to take effect.
  • Rating your pain using the 1 to 10 pain scale. A 0 on the scale means no pain, and a 10 on the scale means the worst pain you can imagine.
  • Reporting your pain as a number helps the doctors and nurses know how well your treatment is working and whether or not to make any changes.
